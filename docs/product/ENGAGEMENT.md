# Learning Engagement

## Product position

NetBite should feel engaging because the learner manipulates a network and sees meaningful consequences. It should not depend on fake terminal noise, excessive animation, or points that do not represent learning.

## Preferred patterns

### Try, respond, explain

Introduce a concept with a tiny interaction, respond immediately, then explain the result. Examples:

- Choose the device that connects nearby computers.
- Connect a PC to the correct shared device.
- Predict the next stop in a packet path.

### Packet-path storytelling

Highlight the active cable and device while a packet moves. Use a short real caption such as `PC 1 SENDS`, `SWITCH FORWARDS`, and `PC 2 RECEIVES`. Do not imply protocol accuracy that the simplified model does not provide.

### Targeted review

- Flashcards support `Know` and `Still learning` classifications.
- Missed quiz concepts can be retried without repeating mastered questions.
- Failed labs reveal hints gradually rather than displaying the solution immediately.

### Meaningful progress

- Preserve the best quiz score as well as completion.
- Resume the next unfinished activity.
- Add streaks, leagues, and broad XP systems only after multiple chapters and durable progress exist.

## Feedback polish

Use restrained haptics for a completed connection, correct answer, or objective completion. Sound may be added later only with a mute setting. Motion, sound, and haptics must never carry information alone.
